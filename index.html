Josh Kim

Masters Student in Mental Health Counseling · Gordon Conwell Theological Seminary

Hi! I'm Josh and I graduated with a bachelor's degree in computer science at the University of Wisconsin Madison in May 2024. I am now pursuing a master's in mental health counseling at Gordon Conwell Theological Seminary. There are three different areas I hope to grow in during my time here:

  1. Spiritual Formation: a time for me to be discipled and spend time with God for my spiritual foundation to be more deeply developed.
  2. Tools for Mission: a time for me to gather the tools to use the gifts God has given me – my journey in getting to know God – in order to bless the people around me.
  3. Healing of Nations: a time for me to get to know different people and discover how God wants to use me as a vessel towards a restored Eden (Revelation 22).



Vision

Spiritual Formation

COVID shook up the mission field, especially on college campuses. For two years, students like myself struggled to pursue community and a relational discipleship during undergrad. Many campus ministries lost their foundation, and as I came into UW-Madison in 2021 post-COVID, the ministries in the area were in a rebuilding period. However, my experience during COVID was different and life changing. In isolation, I got into the habit of spending time with God everyday, and everyday God would grow my heart closer and closer to His. I loved spending time with Him alone, and He grew my heart to go and serve in my upcoming season at Madison. So with this heart I went, and boy did I get wrecked. During my time serving, I realized that all the things God had taught me about loving people and loving Him were SOOO much easier said than done. I am thankful for these experiences because it helped me realize two other things that showed me the urgency to become spiritually formed.

  1. Due to the effects of COVID, there is a setback in discipleship and spiritual development for a lot of folks in this upcoming generation.
  2. I am very ill-equipped.
So as I step into seminary, I am praying that God would continue to develop me further to know His heart better, that I could be a pillar in this world for God to shine. As the world becomes increasingly different by the effects of what we are going through, I can’t help but feel the urgency to know and share God’s heart with the people around me.

Tools For Mission

Now why counseling? During college I spent some time diving into the heart of outreach, which led me to scripture with Jesus and the woman at the well. After her encounter with Jesus, she couldn’t help but share with the towns how Jesus knew everything about her. As I reflected on what my encounter with Jesus was like, it was at a moment where I was brokenhearted and felt unseen. Unknowingly battling depression at an early age with the loss of my mom, Jesus revealed to me that I am seen by Him, and deeply loved. And since then, He has been continuously revealing deeper parts of Himself to me through the avenue of emotional healing. The journey of believing that I was unlovable to realizing that there is an almighty being that says otherwise is a gift I dearly cherish, and want to share with others. I am passionate to help guide people in their own healing journeys in hopes that they may get to know the love of Jesus.

Healing of Nations

As I spent time considering whether to pursue a job in computer science or counseling, God led my heart to take 10 days to intentionally pray on it (inspired by my devotionals in Jeremiah). Everyday I went into my time of devotionals expectant and excited for what God might say, to find no real direction. But it wasn’t until the 10th day, as I was reading through Revelations 22, where something really struck my heart. The healing of nations is a piece that God takes into account when it comes to the kingdom of heaven. God loves His people dearly, so I can’t imagine how much joy it brings Him for us to love one another. As I prayed and meditated that night, my heart for a unified people grew a lot, and I prayed to be a stepping stone in this unified kingdom. Ever since then, God has been placing it in my heart to understand the nooks and crannies of my own Asian American identity, and how that shapes every aspect of who I am. As I grow to figure myself out, I hope to branch out to honor other cultures, admire our differences in celebration, become a stepping stone towards a unified kingdom, and be equipped to guide fellow Asian Americans towards this bigger kingdom.

Partnership Program

What is it?

The Partnership Program is geared to equip future ministers with the ability to fundraise funds for a ministry, build and maintain relationships, and an understanding of biblical stewardship. My participation in the Partnership Program means that I receive a full-tuition scholarship from GCTS. In order to maintain participation within the program means I have to attain certain goals. These goals include me building and maintaining relationships with monthly updates, raising about $15,000 yearly, and taking classes to learn and understand biblical stewardship principles.
